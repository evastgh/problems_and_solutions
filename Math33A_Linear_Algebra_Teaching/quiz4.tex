\noindent1. Consider the basis $\mathcal B = \l\{\tv31, \tv52\r\}$ of $\R^2$.\\
\\
(a) Derive $P$ such that $[x]_{\mathcal B} = Px$ for any $x \in \R^2$. \\
(b) Derive $S$ such that $x = S[x]_{\mathcal B}$ for any $x \in \R^2$.

\noindent2. Derive the matrix $A$ such that $T(x) = Ax$ corresponds to scaling $\tv31$ two times and scaling $\tv52$ three times, i.e. 
$$T\l(\tv31\r) = 2\tv31 = \tv62, \qquad T\l(\tv52\r) = 3\tv52 = \tv{15}6.$$
You don't need to multiply out the matrix. \\
\\
\textbf{Bonus (2 points):} What is the rank of $A$? \textit{Hints:} Instead of computing $rref(A)$, you may want to assess $ker(T)$ and use rank-nullity theorem: $\dim(ker(T)) + rank(A) = 2$.
