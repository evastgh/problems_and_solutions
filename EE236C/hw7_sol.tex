\noindent To show the \textit{linearized ADMM}
\begin{align*}
  x_{k+1} &= \prox_{\sigma f}(x_k - \tau\sigma A^T (Ax_k - y_k + u_k)) \\
  y_{k+1} &= \prox_{\frac1\tau g}(Ax_{k+1} + u_k) \\
  u_{k+1} &= u_k + Ax_{k+1} - y_{k+1}
\end{align*}
is equivalent to \textit{PDHG} applied to the dual problem,
\begin{align*}
  z_{k+1} &= \prox_{\tau g^\ast}(z_k + \tau A\tilde x_k) \\
  \tilde x_{k+1} &= \prox_{\sigma f}(\tilde x_k - \sigma A^T(2z_{k+1}-z_k)),
\end{align*}
first we eliminate the variable $y$ in linearized ADMM by
$u_{k+1} = u_k + Ax_{k+1} - \prox_{\frac1\tau g}(Ax_{k+1} + u_k)$. Using Moreau decomposition on $\/1\tau g$, we can reformulate linearized ADMM as 
\begin{align*}
  x_{k+1} &= \prox_{\sigma f}(x_k - \tau\sigma A^T (Ax_k - y_k + u_k)) \\
  u_{k+1} &= \/1\tau\prox_{\tau g^\ast}(\tau(Ax_{k+1} + u_k)).
\end{align*}
From here consider $z_k = \tau u_k$, $\tilde x_k = x_k$ and start the iteration at $u$-update and renumber the iterates, we get PDHG applied on dual problem.
