\noindent2. Consider an $n\x n$ matrix  $A$ such that $\det(A) = 3$. Answer the following questions. You do not need to provide reasoning to your answers.\\
(a) $\det(A^T) = $ ? \\
(b) $\det(A^T A) = $ ? \\
(c) Consider the QR-factorization $A = QR$. $\det(Q) = $ ?, $\det(R) = $ ? \textit{Hint:} Make observation to the value of $\det(Q^TQ) = \det(I)$ to conclude about $\det(Q)$. \\
\\
3. \textbf{(Bonus, 2 points)} We know that similar matrices have the same value of determinant. Can you give an example such that $\det(A) = \det(B)$ for two matrices $A, B \in \R^{n\x n}$ but $A$ and $B$ are not similar? You have to justify that the matrices you give are not similar.
